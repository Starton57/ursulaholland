SC Real Estate Law

What SC Law Requires
You to Know

South Carolina real estate transactions are governed by clear disclosure and agency laws. As your Realtor®, I ensure every transaction is fully compliant.

Residential Property Disclosure

South Carolina law (S.C. Code § 27-50) requires sellers to complete a Residential Property Condition Disclosure Statement before a contract is signed. This 24-question form covers structural components, HVAC, plumbing, flood history, HOA restrictions, environmental hazards (lead, asbestos, mold), and more. Sellers are solely responsible for completing this form.

Agency Relationship Disclosure

Per S.C. Code § 40-57-370, I am required to provide a meaningful explanation of agency relationships at the first practical opportunity. You may work with me as a client (written agency agreement) or as a customer (limited duties, no advocacy). The choice is yours — South Carolina's real estate consumer choice law puts you in control.

Brokerage Identification

Per S.C. Code § 40-57-135(E)(2), all advertising and communications must clearly identify the full name of the brokerage firm.
